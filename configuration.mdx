KrateCMS gives you control over your site's behavior, appearance, and security entirely through the admin interface. All customer-facing settings live under **Settings** in the left sidebar, organized into categories. This page explains what each category controls and where to find it.

<Note>
  Settings require the **`settings.edit`** permission, which the **admin** and **super-admin** roles hold by default. Managers, editors, and other roles can't open Settings unless a super-admin grants them the permission. If you don't see **Settings** in your sidebar, ask a super-admin for access.
</Note>

## Settings categories

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="General — /admin/settings/general">
    General settings control the foundational identity of your site.

    | Setting | What it does |
    |---|---|
    | **Site Name** | The name displayed in the browser tab, emails, and the admin header |
    | **Site Description** | A short summary used in meta tags and the public homepage |
    | **Footer Text** | Custom text or HTML shown in the site footer |
    | **Company Name** | Your organization's legal or operating name (used in invoices and formal email footers) |
    | **Company Address** | Mailing address used in invoices and email footers where required |

    Set your site name and description first — they appear in every outbound email your site sends and in search engine previews.

    <Note>
      Timezone is platform-managed via the `APP_TIMEZONE` environment variable and is not exposed as a UI field. Contact your platform administrator if you need it changed.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Brand — /admin/settings/brand">
    Brand settings control the entire visual identity of your site. Changes here apply to both the public-facing site and, where applicable, the admin interface.

    | Setting | What it does |
    |---|---|
    | **Primary Color** | The main color used for primary buttons, links, and active states |
    | **Secondary Color** | A complementary tone for surfaces and supporting UI |
    | **Accent Color** | A highlight color for decorative elements and emphasis |
    | **Typography** | Separate heading and body fonts |
    | **Theme Mode** | Light or dark |
    | **Logo** | Uploaded image displayed in the header and emails |
    | **Favicon** | Small icon shown in browser tabs and bookmarks |
    | **Custom CSS** | Freeform CSS injected into every public page for fine-grained overrides |

    <Tip>
      Use Custom CSS for tweaks that the color and typography controls do not cover — for example, adjusting spacing around a specific element or overriding a default font weight.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Menus — /admin/settings/menus">
    The menu builder controls your public header and footer navigation. Add links to your pages, built-in site routes, and external URLs; set per-item visibility; and reorder them. This replaces the old "Posts in Nav" toggle.

    See [Navigation menus](/settings/menus) for the full guide. On by default.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Content — /admin/settings/content">
    Content settings determine how your site presents content to visitors.

    **Site Visibility** controls who can read your site:

    | Value | Behavior |
    |---|---|
    | **Public** | All published content is readable by anyone, no login required |
    | **Members Only** | Visitors must be logged in to read any content |

    **Homepage Type** controls what appears at the root URL of your site:

    | Value | Behavior |
    |---|---|
    | **Posts** | A scrolling feed of published posts with category filters (default) |
    | **Page** | A specific Page you select from the page picker — useful for custom landing pages |
    | **Marketing** | A legacy marketing template |
    | **Krate page** | A link-in-bio landing page (bio, social links, content buttons, and latest posts), configured at **Settings → Krate page**. See [Krate page](/content/krate-page) |

    The public header and footer navigation is curated separately in the [menu builder](/settings/menus) at **Settings → Menus** — there is no "Posts in Nav" toggle on this page. To show a Posts link in your navigation, add an **Internal link** menu item pointing at the posts archive (`/posts`).

    <Note>
      Site visibility is a site-wide gate. Individual posts also have their own visibility setting (public or members-only), which combines with the site-wide setting — a members-only post is always private regardless of site visibility.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Krate page — /admin/settings/krate-page">
    Configure your link-in-bio [Krate page](/content/krate-page): avatar, bio, social links, content buttons, and which recent posts to feature. It takes effect when your homepage type is set to **Krate page**.

    Tenant sites only. On by default.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Analytics — platform-managed during alpha">
    Tracking and analytics tags (Google Tag Manager, etc.) are configured at the **platform** level during the KrateCMS alpha period and are not available as a tenant-facing settings panel. Platform tags render across the central marketing site and every tenant subdomain.

    <Warning>
      The tenant `/admin/settings/analytics` route is blocked by middleware during alpha — tenants do not see this entry in the sidebar. Per-tenant analytics configuration will be exposed in a future release. For now, ask your platform administrator if you need a tag added.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Contact — /admin/settings/contact">
    Contact settings configure the public contact form that appears on your site. Use these to control whether the form is active, where submissions are sent, and how emails are labeled.

    | Setting | What it does |
    |---|---|
    | **Enable Contact Form** | Show or hide the public contact form |
    | **Recipient Email** | The address where contact form submissions are delivered |
    | **Subject Prefix** | A short label prepended to every contact email subject line (for example, `[My Site]`) |

    Submissions are also logged in the admin at `/admin/contact-submissions` so you can review them even if an email is missed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Security — /admin/settings/security">
    Security settings let you enforce authentication and session policies for everyone who logs into your site.

    | Setting | What it does |
    |---|---|
    | **Max Login Attempts** | Number of failed logins before an account is locked out |
    | **Lockout Duration** | How long (in minutes) a locked account must wait before retrying |
    | **Password Policy** | Minimum length and complexity requirements for all user passwords |
    | **Session Timeout** | Idle time (in minutes) before a user is automatically logged out |

    <Tip>
      A lockout duration of 15–30 minutes and a max of 5 attempts is a common starting point. For high-security environments, consider tightening to 3 attempts and a 60-minute lockout.
    </Tip>

    KrateCMS also automatically rejects passwords found in data breaches via the Have I Been Pwned database — this protection is always on and cannot be disabled.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Chat — /admin/settings/chat">
    Chat settings configure the AI assistant that powers the chat interface, the image generator, and the API Q&A mode.

    | Setting | What it does |
    |---|---|
    | **AI Chat Model** | The OpenAI model used for chat responses (for example, `gpt-4o`, `gpt-4o-mini`) |
    | **Model Fallbacks** | Ordered list of fallback models if the primary model is unavailable |
    | **Temperature** | Controls response creativity; range 0–2, where 0 is deterministic and 2 is most creative |
    | **Max Tokens** | The maximum length of each AI response |
    | **HTTP Timeout** | Seconds to wait for an OpenAI response before failing |
    | **HTTP Retries** | Number of automatic retries on transient API failures |
    | **HTTP Retry Delay (ms)** | Milliseconds to wait between retries |
    | **Verify SSL** | Whether to validate SSL certificates on outbound API calls |
    | **AI Image Model** | The OpenAI model used for image generation (for example, `dall-e-3`) |
    | **AI Image Quality** | Standard or HD output quality |
    | **AI Image HTTP Timeout** | Seconds to wait for an image generation response |
    | **API Read Allowed Hosts** | Allowlist of external hostnames the API Q&A mode is permitted to read |

    <Note>
      The Chat settings panel is gated by a feature flag. If you do not see it in your sidebar, the AI chat feature has not been enabled on your plan.
    </Note>

    <Warning>
      If a setting in the Chat panel appears grayed out and labeled "overridden by environment," it has been locked by your platform operator and cannot be changed from the UI.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Billing — /admin/settings/billing">
    Billing settings let you view your current subscription, update your payment method, and access invoice history. All payment processing is handled by Stripe — your card details are never stored in KrateCMS.

    From the Billing page you can:

    - Check your current plan and billing period
    - Update your payment method via Stripe's secure hosted page
    - Download PDF invoices for past charges

    <Warning>
      The Billing panel is gated by a feature flag and is off by default during early access. Contact your platform administrator to enable Stripe billing for your site.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Custom Domains — /admin/settings/domains">
    Connect your own domain to your site, verify it with DNS records, and choose a primary address. See [Custom domains](/settings/custom-domains).

    Tenant sites only. Limited to the **admin** and **super-admin** roles; on by default.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Settings priority

When a setting can be configured both through the admin UI and through a platform-level environment variable, the environment variable always wins. If a field is grayed out in the UI with a note that it is "overridden by environment," it means your platform operator has locked that value. Contact them to change it.

## Quick reference

| Category | URL | Access | Feature Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| General | `/admin/settings/general` | `settings.edit` | Always on |
| Brand | `/admin/settings/brand` | `settings.edit` | Always on |
| Menus | `/admin/settings/menus` | `settings.edit` | On by default |
| Content | `/admin/settings/content` | `settings.edit` | Always on |
| Krate page | `/admin/settings/krate-page` | `settings.edit` | On by default (tenant only) |
| Security | `/admin/settings/security` | `settings.edit` | Always on |
| Contact | `/admin/settings/contact` | `settings.edit` | On by default |
| Custom Domains | `/admin/settings/domains` | admin / super-admin | On by default (tenant only) |
| Analytics | (platform-managed during alpha) | — | Not exposed to tenants |
| Chat | `/admin/settings/chat` | `settings.edit` | Off by default |
| Billing | `/admin/settings/billing` | `settings.billing` | Off by default |

By default, the `settings.edit` and `settings.billing` permissions are granted only to the **admin** and **super-admin** roles, so Settings is effectively limited to those roles unless a super-admin grants the permission to another role.
