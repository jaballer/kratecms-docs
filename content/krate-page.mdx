A Krate page is a shareable landing page, similar to Linktree. When set as your homepage, your root URL (`/`) shows a compact, mobile-friendly, chromeless profile: your avatar, a short bio, a row of social icons, a stack of link buttons, and — the part a plain link-in-bio tool can't do — your latest posts, rendered right on the page.

Your Krate page is always available at the permanent URL `/links`, regardless of which homepage mode you're using. When the Krate page is your homepage, `/` and `/links` both serve it (with the canonical URL pointing at `/`). When you switch to a different homepage mode, your Krate page stays live at `/links` — so you can share that URL in your Instagram bio, business card, or a DM and it always works.

It's the lowest-effort way to put a shareable presence online. The page renders without the site header or footer navigation, keeping the focus on your profile and links.

The page is search-engine friendly and shareable out of the box — it renders structured data (Person or LocalBusiness JSON-LD, depending on whether you've set a business address) and social-card metadata, so links to it preview nicely on other platforms.

## Turn on the Krate page

The Krate page is one of your [homepage modes](/content/homepage). Switching to it takes two steps: select it as your homepage, then fill in your profile.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Krate page as your homepage">
    Go to **Admin → Settings → Content** (`/admin/settings/content`). In **Homepage Settings**, choose **Krate page** from the Homepage dropdown and click **Save**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Krate page settings">
    Go to **Admin → Settings → Krate page** (`/admin/settings/krate-page`) to fill in your profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in your profile">
    Add your identity details, avatar, social links, content links, and choose how your latest posts appear (see [Configure your Krate page](#configure-your-krate-page) below).
  </Step>
  <Step title="View it live">
    Use the **View live** button on the settings page (or just visit `/`) to see your Krate page as visitors will. Your Krate page is also always available at `/links`, even if you later switch to a different homepage mode. The first time your Krate page goes from empty to having real content — when it's also your homepage — saving shows a brief "You're live!" confirmation with a link straight to your public page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The Krate page is available to **admins** and **super-admins** and is enabled by default. If you don't see **Krate page** in the Homepage dropdown or a **Krate page** entry under Settings, the feature may have been turned off for your site — contact your platform administrator.
</Note>

## Configure your Krate page

All Krate page content is edited at **Settings → Krate page** (`/admin/settings/krate-page`), organized into the sections below.

### Identity

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Display name** | An optional override for the name shown under your avatar. Up to 120 characters. When blank, the Krate page uses your business name from [Business Info](/settings/general#business-info). |
| **Tagline** | A short line under your name. Up to 160 characters. |
| **Bio** | A longer description. Up to 500 characters. Line breaks you enter are preserved on the page. |

The accent color for link buttons and the avatar ring comes from your site-wide [brand accent color](/settings/brand#colors) — set it once in **Settings → Brand** and it applies everywhere, including the Krate page.

### Avatar

Upload a profile photo (JPEG, PNG, or WebP, up to 2 MB). The settings page shows a live preview of the image you select before you save. If you don't upload one, the page shows your initials on a colored background instead.

<Tip>
  To remove your current avatar, tick the **remove** checkbox and save. If you select a new file, it always takes priority over the remove option — so you can swap your photo in a single save.
</Tip>

### Contact, business hours, and address

Contact details, business hours, and your business address are managed from a separate **Business Info** page at **Settings → Business Info** (`/admin/settings/business-info`). The Krate page settings include display toggles to control which of these sections appear on your public page:

| Toggle | What it controls |
|---|---|
| **Show contact** | Tap-to-email and tap-to-call buttons (only appear when an email or phone is set in Business Info). |
| **Show business hours** | A structured opening-hours table, Monday through Sunday. |
| **Show address** | Your business address with a "Get directions" link to Google Maps. |

<Note>
  Business Info is always available in the admin — it is not gated behind the Krate page feature flag, because it also feeds your transactional email footers. See [Business Info](/settings/general#business-info) for the full field reference.
</Note>

When a business address is present, the Krate page's structured data upgrades from `Person` to `LocalBusiness` JSON-LD, including your postal address, phone, email, and opening hours.

<Note>
  Business hours support one interval per day — a single open–close range, with no overnight spans (the close time must be later than the open time). Split shifts and an "open now" indicator are planned for a later release.
</Note>

### Social links

Add up to 20 social profiles. For each one, pick a **platform** and paste your profile **URL** (must start with `http` or `https`). Supported platforms:

- Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn
- X (formerly Twitter)
- YouTube, TikTok, SoundCloud, GitHub
- Website (a generic link for anything else)

Each platform renders its own icon in the social row. Use the **Move up** and **Move down** buttons to reorder your links — the order you set is the order the icons appear in.

### Content links

Add up to 50 link buttons — these are the heart of a Krate page. For each one, set:

- **Label** — the button text (up to 120 characters)
- **URL** — where it points (must start with `http` or `https`)
- **Description** — an optional one-line subtitle (up to 200 characters)

Use the up and down arrows to reorder your buttons. They appear on the page in the order you set.

### Latest posts

Show your most recent published posts inline, beneath your links.

| Setting | Description |
|---|---|
| **Show recent posts** | Toggle the posts block on or off. |
| **Number of posts** | How many to show, from 1 to 10 (default 3). |
| **Category** | Optionally limit the block to one category (text, audio, or video). |
| **Show excerpts** | Whether each post shows its excerpt under the title. |

The posts block respects each post's own visibility — drafts and members-only posts never appear to logged-out visitors.

## Edit your Krate page in one click

When you're logged in with permission to edit settings and you visit your Krate page (at `/` or `/links`), an **Edit Krate Page** button appears in the admin bar at the top of the screen. It takes you straight to the Krate page settings — the same shortcut the **Edit Post** and **Edit Page** buttons give you elsewhere.

## Visibility on members-only sites

If your site is set to **Members only**, your Krate page is still public — it's designed to be your front door, so locking it behind a login would defeat the purpose. The profile, links, and social icons stay visible to everyone.

Your **posts** remain protected: the Latest posts block still hides members-only posts and drafts from logged-out visitors, so only content you've made public shows there.

<Note>
  The Krate page is one of four [homepage modes](/content/homepage). You can switch back to a posts feed, a Page, or the marketing template at any time from **Settings → Content** — your Krate page profile is kept, so switching back later restores it. Even when it's not your homepage, your Krate page stays accessible at `/links`.
</Note>
