KrateCMS lets you generate a featured image for any post without leaving the editor. Powered by OpenAI's image generation models, the tool turns a plain-text prompt into a ready-to-use image that is automatically attached to your post and stored in your site's cloud storage.

## Opening the image generator

You can generate a featured image when creating a new post or editing an existing one.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the post editor">
    Go to **Posts** in the admin sidebar, then click **New Post** to create one, or click the title of an existing post to open it for editing.

    The editor is available at `/admin/posts/create` for new posts and `/admin/posts/{slug}/edit` for existing ones.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Featured Image section">
    Scroll down past the main content area to the **Featured Image** section in the post editor sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the AI generate button">
    Click the **Generate with AI** button (or similar label) to open the image generation interface. A prompt field appears where you can describe the image you want.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your prompt">
    Type a description of the image. Be as specific as possible — include the subject, visual style, color palette, and mood for best results.

    ```text
    A minimalist flat-design illustration of a rocket launching, blue and purple gradient background, clean vector style
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate and review">
    Click **Generate**. The AI produces an image based on your prompt, which previews immediately in the Featured Image section. If you are not satisfied with the result, click **Regenerate** to try again with the same prompt or update your description first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the post">
    Once you are happy with the image, save or publish your post. The generated image is automatically set as the featured image and stored in your tenant's cloud storage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Writing effective prompts

The quality of the generated image depends heavily on how clearly you describe what you want. Vague prompts produce generic results; specific prompts produce images that fit your content.

<Tip>
  Include three key elements in every prompt: the **subject** (what the image shows), the **style** (illustration, photograph, flat design, etc.), and the **mood or color palette** (warm tones, dark and moody, bright and minimal). For example: "A cozy home office desk with a laptop and coffee mug, warm afternoon light, photorealistic style."
</Tip>

### What to avoid

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Overly abstract prompts">
    Prompts like "success" or "technology" give the AI too little to work with and produce generic stock-photo results. Add concrete visual details — objects, settings, styles — to guide the output.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Conflicting style instructions">
    Mixing incompatible styles (e.g., "photorealistic watercolor illustration") can produce inconsistent results. Pick one primary style and stick to it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Text or logos in the image">
    AI image models do not reliably render readable text or accurate logos. If you need text in your featured image, add it in a separate image editor after generating the base image.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Uploading your own image instead

If you prefer to use a custom image rather than generating one, you can upload directly from the same **Featured Image** section. Click the upload option, select a file from your computer, and it will be stored in your tenant's cloud storage and set as the featured image — the same as a generated image.

## Storage and reuse

Generated images are stored in your tenant's cloud storage automatically. You do not need to download and re-upload them. Each generated image is tied to the post it was created for — if you need the same image on another post, generate a new one using the same prompt or upload the downloaded file manually.

<Note>
  AI image generation availability depends on your plan and site configuration. If the **Generate with AI** button does not appear in the Featured Image section, contact your site administrator to check whether the feature is enabled for your account.
</Note>
