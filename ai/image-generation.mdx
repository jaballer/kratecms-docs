---
title: "Generate AI featured images directly in the post editor"
description: "Generate a featured image for any post from its own content, right inside the KrateCMS post editor — no design tools and no prompt-writing required."
---

KrateCMS can generate a featured image for a post directly from the editor. Instead of asking you to write an image prompt, it reads the post's **title and content** and produces a relevant image — so you write your post, click one button, and KrateCMS creates and attaches the image for you. It's powered by OpenAI's image models and stores the result in your site's cloud storage.

<Note>
  This is the in-post featured-image generator. It's different from the **Images** mode in [AI chat](/ai/chat), where you generate standalone marketing images from a text prompt.
</Note>

## Before you start

Two things to know about how this works:

- **Generation is based on your post's content**, not a prompt you type. Write (or paste) your post body first — the button stays disabled until the post has some content.
- **The post must be saved first.** The generator lives on the post editor for an existing post. If you're writing something new, save it as a draft, then generate the image from the editor.

## Generate an image

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a saved post in the editor">
    Go to **Posts** in the admin sidebar and click the post you want to edit. (For a new post, fill in the title and body and save it as a draft first, then continue editing it.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your content">
    Make sure the post has a title and body — the image is generated from them. The richer and clearer your content, the more relevant the result.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Featured Image section">
    Scroll to the **Featured Image** section in the editor sidebar. Below the upload field you'll see a **Generate with AI** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Generate with AI">
    Click **Generate with AI** and confirm. If the post already has a featured image, the confirmation warns you that the new image will replace it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for it to finish">
    Generation runs in the background and usually takes 30–60 seconds. The button shows a **Generating…** spinner with an elapsed-time counter while it works.

    <Warning>
      Keep the editor open while the image generates — don't navigate away until it finishes.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the result">
    When it's done, the image appears in the Featured Image section and KrateCMS fills in the alt text for you. It's already attached to the post. Not quite right? Click **Regenerate with AI** to produce a new one from the same content. If generation fails or times out, you'll see an error and your existing image (if any) is left untouched.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Getting better results

Because the image is derived from your post, the way to improve it is to improve the post — there's no prompt to tune.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Write the content before generating">
    The generator reads your title and body, so generate the image after you've written a solid draft rather than on an empty post. A descriptive opening gives the model more to work with than a one-line stub.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use a clear, specific title">
    The title carries a lot of weight. A concrete title ("Migrating a Laravel app to Postgres") produces a more focused image than a vague one ("An update").
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Regenerate if the first result misses">
    Generation has natural variation. If the first image isn't right, **Regenerate with AI** runs again from the same content and usually returns something different.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Text and logos won't render reliably">
    AI image models don't reproduce readable text or accurate logos. Treat the result as a visual backdrop; if you need words or a logo on the image, add them in an image editor afterward and upload the result.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Upload your own image instead

If you'd rather use a specific image, upload one directly in the same **Featured Image** section instead of generating. Choose a file from your device (JPEG, PNG, or WebP, up to 2 MB) and you'll see a live preview before you save. Your upload is stored in your site's cloud storage, exactly like a generated image.

## Storage and reuse

Generated images are stored in your site's cloud storage automatically — you don't need to download and re-upload them. Each image is attached to the post it was created for. To use a similar image on another post, generate one from that post's content, or upload a file manually.

<Note>
  AI image generation depends on your plan and site configuration. If you don't see the **Generate with AI** button in the Featured Image section, the feature hasn't been enabled for your site — contact your platform administrator.
</Note>
