KrateCMS includes a built-in AI chat powered by OpenAI, accessible directly from your admin panel. You can use it to brainstorm ideas, draft and refine content, or switch into agent mode and let the AI take real actions on your posts — creating, searching, fetching, and updating content on your behalf.

## Opening AI chat

Navigate to **AI Chat** in the admin sidebar. The chat interface loads immediately — no additional configuration is required on your end. If you do not see the AI Chat option in your sidebar, the feature may not be enabled on your plan. Contact your site administrator for access.

## Chat modes

KrateCMS AI chat offers six modes. Pick a mode from the selector at the top of the chat panel — each one tunes the assistant for a different task. You can switch between modes within the same session.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Chat">
    The default conversational interface. Use it to ask questions, get writing feedback, brainstorm topics, or generate draft text you can copy into your posts.

    **Example prompts:**

    ```text
    Write me a 150-word introduction for a blog post about remote team culture.
    ```

    ```text
    Give me five title ideas for a post announcing our new pricing plans.
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Email Copy">
    Generates email marketing copy — subject lines, body copy, calls to action — that you can paste straight into your email tool.

    ```text
    Write 5 subject lines for a flash sale on summer apparel.
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Images">
    Generates marketing images from a text prompt. Images appear in the chat panel; for the in-post featured-image generator, see [AI image generation](/ai/image-generation).

    ```text
    Hero image for a summer campaign — bright, cheerful, beach setting.
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Code">
    Generates short HTML and code snippets for marketing use — buttons, banners, embeds, small components.

    ```text
    HTML button that says "Shop Now" with rounded corners and a hover state.
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="API Q&A">
    Lets you ask questions about JSON data from approved external URLs. Reference an endpoint inline with the `@` syntax — for example, `What's in @https://api.example.com/v1/products?` — and the assistant fetches the URL and answers from the JSON. Allowed hosts are configured by the platform operator under **Settings → Chat → API Read Allowed Hosts**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Agent">
    Agent mode gives the AI the ability to take actions inside your KrateCMS site. Instead of just generating text, the AI can interact with your posts directly through tool-calling.

    **Actions available in agent mode:**

    - **List posts** — retrieve a summary of your existing posts
    - **Search posts** — find posts by keyword or topic
    - **Get a post** — pull up the full content of a post by id or slug
    - **Create a post** — the AI drafts and saves a post (requires `posts.create` permission)
    - **Update a post** — modify the content of an existing post (requires `posts.edit` permission)

    **Example prompts:**

    ```text
    List my last 5 published posts.
    ```

    ```text
    Search for posts about product launches.
    ```

    ```text
    Create a new draft post titled "Year in Review" with an introduction about our team's highlights.
    ```

    <Note>
      Agent mode actions are real — they create and update actual posts in your CMS. Review the AI's proposed action before confirming it, and check the resulting post before publishing. Tool availability depends on your role: contributors and authors see only the read-only tools (list, search, get).
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Conversation history and token usage

Your conversation history is maintained for the duration of your session. You can scroll back through the full exchange to review earlier messages or copy generated content.

Token usage for every message is recorded behind the scenes. Inline counts are not shown in the chat panel itself — to review per-message token usage and model costs, super-admins can open **Admin → Conversations** and inspect any conversation.

## Tenant scope

The AI agent operates entirely within your tenant's content. It can only see and act on the posts that belong to your site — it cannot access other tenants' data or take actions outside your account.

## Tips for better results

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be specific with your prompts">
    The more context you give, the more useful the response. Instead of "write a blog post," try "write a 300-word blog post introduction about the benefits of async communication for remote engineering teams, written for a non-technical audience."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use chat mode to draft, then copy into the editor">
    AI chat is a great place to generate raw content. Once you have something you like, copy it into the post editor and refine it before publishing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="In agent mode, confirm before publishing">
    When the AI creates or updates a post, it saves as a draft by default. Open the post editor to review, adjust, and set a publish date before making it live.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Iterate on responses">
    If the first response isn't quite right, follow up with a refinement. For example: "Make that shorter" or "Change the tone to be more formal" — the AI retains the conversation context.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
