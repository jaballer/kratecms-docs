---
title: "Serve your KrateCMS site from your own domain"
description: "Attach a custom domain (like your-brand.com) to your KrateCMS tenant with automatic SSL via Cloudflare — no DNS expertise required."
---

By default, your KrateCMS site lives at a subdomain on the platform (for example, `yoursite.kratecms.com`). When you're ready to launch under your own brand, attach a custom domain — visitors will see your URL in the address bar, and you'll get a Cloudflare-issued SSL certificate automatically.

<Note>
Custom domains are a tenant-only feature, gated by a platform feature flag. If you don't see **Domains** under **Settings**, contact your platform administrator to enable it on your site.
</Note>

## Getting there

Navigate to **Admin → Settings → Domains** (`/admin/settings/domains`). The page requires admin or super-admin access.

## How it works

KrateCMS uses Cloudflare for SaaS to handle custom domains. The flow is:

1. You enter your domain (for example, `your-brand.com` or `www.your-brand.com`).
2. KrateCMS registers the domain with Cloudflare and returns DNS records you must add at your registrar.
3. You copy those records into your DNS provider (GoDaddy, Namecheap, Cloudflare, Route 53, etc.).
4. KrateCMS polls Cloudflare for the verification status; once the records propagate, your domain is active and serving traffic over HTTPS.

You do not need to install certificates or configure a reverse proxy — Cloudflare handles SSL termination and routes verified traffic to the platform.

## Attach a domain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Domains settings">
    Go to **Admin → Settings → Domains**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your hostname">
    Type the full hostname you want to serve from this tenant — for example, `your-brand.com` or `blog.your-brand.com`. Click **Attach**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the DNS records">
    KrateCMS displays the verification records returned by Cloudflare (a TXT or CNAME record, depending on your setup). Copy them into your DNS provider's control panel exactly as shown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for verification">
    DNS propagation usually takes minutes, but can take up to 24 hours. The Domains page shows the current verification status — refresh periodically until it reads **Active**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make it primary (optional)">
    Once active, you can mark a domain as **Primary**. The primary domain is used for canonical URLs in emails and SEO meta tags. Non-primary active domains still serve traffic — useful for handling both apex and `www.` variants.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Domain statuses

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | The domain has been registered with Cloudflare; KrateCMS is waiting for DNS records to propagate and Cloudflare to confirm. |
| **Active** | DNS is verified and SSL is issued. The domain is serving traffic. |
| **Expired** | A previously active domain has had its DNS records removed or its Cloudflare hostname invalidated. Re-add the DNS records to restore. |
| **Failed** | Cloudflare could not verify ownership. Confirm your DNS records match what's shown in the Domains page and try again. |

KrateCMS polls Cloudflare in the background to keep these statuses up to date. You don't need to refresh manually — the page updates as transitions happen.

## Manage attached domains

The Domains page lists every domain attached to your tenant. For each row you can:

- **Set Primary** — make this domain the canonical one for emails, OG meta tags, and links inside the admin.
- **Remove** — detach the domain from your tenant. Cloudflare's hostname record is cleaned up automatically.

<Warning>
Removing your primary domain takes effect immediately. Visitors who bookmarked the removed URL will get a 404 until they switch back to the platform subdomain or another active domain.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My domain has been pending for hours">
    DNS propagation can take up to 24 hours in some cases, especially if you previously had a different `CNAME` or `A` record on the same hostname. Use a tool like [dnschecker.org](https://dnschecker.org) to see whether your new records are visible to public resolvers. If they are visible but the status hasn't flipped, contact your platform administrator — they can ask Cloudflare to re-verify.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I got a failed status — what went wrong?">
    The most common causes are:

    - The TXT or CNAME record was copied with extra whitespace, a missing dot, or a typo.
    - The record points at a different host than what KrateCMS displays.
    - Your registrar adds a domain suffix automatically (e.g., entering `your-brand.com` becomes `your-brand.com.your-brand.com`).

    Double-check the records in your DNS provider against what's shown in **Settings → Domains**. Many providers offer a "raw" or "advanced" record editor that avoids these auto-suffix problems.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use both my apex and www.?">
    Yes. Attach both `your-brand.com` and `www.your-brand.com` as separate domain rows. Set one as primary; KrateCMS will serve traffic from both. Use a 301 redirect at your DNS or web layer if you want one to canonicalize to the other.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does my SSL certificate auto-renew?">
    Yes. Cloudflare issues and renews the SSL certificate automatically for the life of the attached domain. There's no expiry to monitor as long as the domain is in Active status.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
