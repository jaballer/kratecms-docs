The General settings page is where you set the foundational identity of your site. The details you enter here appear in the browser tab, throughout the admin UI, in outgoing emails, and at the bottom of every page — so it's worth getting them right before you start publishing content.

## Getting there

Navigate to **Settings → General** in your admin sidebar, or go directly to `/admin/settings/general`.

## Settings overview

| Setting | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Site name** | The name of your site. Appears in the browser tab title, in outgoing notification emails, and in various places across the admin UI. |
| **Site description** | A short summary of your site. Used in meta tags and wherever a brief site summary is needed. |
| **Footer text** | The text that appears at the bottom of every page on your public site. Commonly used for copyright notices or short taglines. |
| **Timezone** | Controls how post publish dates and all timestamps are displayed across the admin and the public site. |

## Updating your settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open General settings">
    In the admin sidebar, click **Settings**, then click **General**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit the fields">
    Update any combination of **Site name**, **Site description**, **Footer text**, and **Timezone** as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save** to apply. Changes take effect immediately — no restart required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Site name

Your site name is used in more places than it might seem. Wherever KrateCMS needs to reference your site — in the browser tab, in the header of notification emails, and in the admin dashboard heading — it pulls from this field. Keep it concise and recognizable.

<Note>
If you rename your site, the new name appears immediately in the admin UI and in all emails sent after the change. Previously sent emails are not affected.
</Note>

## Timezone

The timezone setting determines how all dates and times are displayed on your site. This includes post publish dates shown to readers, the timestamps on scheduled posts in the admin, and activity logs.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Why it matters">
    If your site timezone is set to UTC but your audience is in New York, a post published at 9:00 PM UTC will show as published at 9:00 PM — even though it's actually 5:00 PM Eastern. Setting the correct timezone keeps your publish schedule and displayed dates consistent with your expectations.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="How to set it">
    In the **Timezone** dropdown, search for or scroll to your region and city (for example, `America/New_York` or `Europe/London`). Select it and click **Save**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
If you manage content for a global audience, set your timezone to match where your editorial team is based — this keeps your publish schedules predictable for the people actually scheduling posts.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where does the site name appear publicly?">
    The site name appears in the browser tab title on every page of your site, and in the subject line and header of system emails (such as password reset and email verification messages). It does not automatically appear as a visible heading on your homepage — that is controlled by your homepage content.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does changing the timezone affect existing post timestamps?">
    Yes. The timezone is applied when displaying timestamps, so changing it will cause all existing post dates and times to display in the new timezone. No data is modified — only how the stored timestamps are presented.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use HTML in the footer text?">
    No — the footer text field is plain text only. If you need custom HTML in your footer, use the Custom CSS field in [Brand settings](/settings/brand) for visual adjustments, or contact your site administrator for structural footer changes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
